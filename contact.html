Attorney
Writer & Aesthete

 I am a corporate lawyer, who recently moved to Nora Ephron's mythic Upper West Side—where the hot chocolate are abundant and the baby strollers, dangerous. I work at a law firm, where I am forced to trade flannel-and-denim for suit-and-tie.

 In my younger and sprightlier days, I dreamt of becoming a writer. I trekked East and graduated college with a fairly useless degree in history and economics from Harvard. My years in Massachusetts found me buried in the dark corners of street cafés and cobwebbed bookstores, where I often waxed poetic on art history, existentialism, and philosophies on love. (I want to punch my old self in the face.) After a brief dalliance in the art museum space and in Paris for a year, I decided to pursue law—for more practical reasons, which I won't bore you with now.

 After graduating from law school at Stanford, I've waylaid my childhood ambition of becoming a writer for the sake of mastering corporate contracts.

About

This is a story about a young man starting his life in New York City. After graduating from law school in California, he decides to uproot his life; move into a small shoebox apartment in the Upper West Side; and begin work at a large law firm. This blog chronicles the laughs, the law, and the love he encounters along the way.
